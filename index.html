About Me

I am a current Computer Science student at Columbia University. I am a research assistant at The GILMLab under Professor Corey Toler-Franklin. I focus on the intersection of quantum mechanics and machine learning. In particular, our project uses quantum mechanics to model molecular and atomic-level interactions for biology and other applications.

I was also a DREAM Fellow at the University of Illinois, working under Professor Sterman. My research consisted of analyzing large datasets of introductory computer science students to determine when a person is considering debugging. I found a significant increase in edits per compilation when students use print and try-catch statements, which may indicate debugging.

Outside of academics, I'm an active Super Smash Brothers player. I am top 15 in New York and, formerly, top 5 in Chicago! I also played chess, reaching a peak USCF rating of 1809. Outside of this, I love to think and talk about hard problems, read, and explore new areas.
